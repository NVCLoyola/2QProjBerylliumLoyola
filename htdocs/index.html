How Should We Face Face to Face?

Introduction

Our world was shocked on December 16, 2019 to the find the emergence of an unknown disease in Wuhan, China. From its origins in China, it spread all around the globe. In the Philippines, classes were suspended in some locations on March 09, 2020 and Luzon-wide quarantine was declared on March 16, 2020 because of this disease which we know now as the Covid-19 pandemic. More class suspensions and community quarantines are declared nationwide causing education to a halt. After some months of dormancy, classes were reopened by DepEd on October 5, 2020.

These classes were conducted via distance learning which can be divided into two methods - modular and online learning. Even though classes returned, it can't be considered successful since a survey concluded that majority of students claim that they learned less with distance learning when compared to face to face (f2f). These dismal results along with international pressure (most countries already opened schools ever since the pandemic) caused DepEd to start plotting f2f classes as early as January 2022. But before f2f reopens, let's have a recap about the advantages of each type.
