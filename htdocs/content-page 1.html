How Should We Face Face to Face?

Two Countries We Shouldn't Follow and What Can We Learn From Them

Israel’s vaccination and Covid response is praised worldwide but they reimplemented f2f classes immediately in a very lax way causing a steep rise in Covid cases that included students and teachers. In fact, policies are so lax and rushed to the point that social distancing is impossible since classrooms are filled to their usual capacity. If there is something we can learn from Israel, it is the fact that we must not rush our reimplementation of f2f classes since doing so will launch a myriad of problems.

Sweden’s Covid response is nothing short of experimental because it didn’t declare a lockdown, implemented optional but recommended wearing of face masks and social distancing, and the government even threatened to punish parents that didn’t want to send their kids to school! Authorities cite that the experimental strategy is said to align with the government’s focus on personal choice, economic reasons, and the populace’s development of “collective immunity”. Though there isn’t a strong rise of Covid cases in the country, it has a high death rate up to 10x higher than its Norwegian neighbor. Unlike Sweden, we must still impose strict lockdown measures for a safe return of f2f classes.

Two Countries We Should Follow and What Can We Learn From Them

Japan took a conservative approach in reimplementing f2f classes. Classes are only half full since students attend class in person on alternating days. As a part of their conservative approach, students are also socially distanced and they undergo daily temperature checks. Even if Japan is a role model when it comes to the rules, the Japanese people aren't exactly the best role models in following government protocols. This is the case since the populace is still hesitant about the otherwise effective protocols.

Of the four countries in this list, Uruguay is the best role model due to a variety of factors. The first thing to keep in mind is its slowly but safely reimplementation of classes. Uruguay has protocols similar to Japan especially the implementation of alternating schedule of in-person and virtual instruction to reduce the number of students in classrooms at one time. Another thing to consider is its efficient lockdowns and safety protocols that made reimplementation of f2f classes very safe. Lastly, it has an obedient populace which makes implementation of protocols easy so it is a better role model than Japan.
