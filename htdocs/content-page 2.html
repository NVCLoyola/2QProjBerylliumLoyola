How Should We Face Face to Face?

Philippine F2F Classes Reimplementation Plan

Duterte has approved the reimplementation of face-to-face classes in areas deemed low risk by DOH with up to 120 schools to join a two-month pilot reopening run. The pilot will cover 100 public schools and 20 private institutions with class size limited to 12 learners in kindergarten, 16 in Grades 1-3, and 20 at Senior Highschool. Aside from being low risked areas, these schools must have passed the safety assessment using the school safety assessment tool of DepEd and have the support of the local government unit in the form of a resolution or letter of support. This pilot isn't full-on f2f since it will be a combination of face-to-face classes in school and distance learning modalities for two months. The aforementioned classes shall be conducted half-day every other week, with participating schools ensuring that class schedules are arranged equitably so that all qualified learners have the opportunity to attend face-to-face classes.

On the other hand, the Commision on Higher Education (CHED) said in-person classes are currently available for certain degree programs like tourism and medical degrees in areas under modified general community quarantine (MGCQ). CHED also stated that f2f classes may soon be allowed regardless of the student's course if the school is located in an area with very low COVID-19 prevalence and a very high vaccination rate. The aforementioned pilot testing will initially be conducted from November 15 to January 31, but pressure from some senators caused DepEd to end pilot testing by December 17. The pilot testing can be considerd a success since no Covid-19 cases are recorded and data-gathering was conducted triumphantly.
